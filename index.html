01 · The Problem

Two systems that can't talk
to each other.

The Marriott Help site and the contact center operate in silos. A guest can spend 10 minutes browsing help articles, initiate a call — and the agent picks up completely blind. That disconnect destroys NPS and makes guests feel like a number, not a member.

Today · Without Data Cloud

  • Guest browses help site for 10+ minutes — none of that is visible to the agent
  • Agent asks "What can I help you with?" — guest just explained it via the help flow
  • No Bonvoy number, no loyalty tier, no stay history passed on connection
  • Guest routes through IVR as if they just picked up the phone cold
  • No visibility into which help articles failed, which tiles were clicked
  • NPS tanks because the guest feels unrecognized and has to repeat themselves

Future State · With Data Cloud

  • Agent knows exactly which pages the guest visited and where they got stuck
  • First words from agent: "I saw you were looking to add a person to your reservation — I can help with that right now"
  • Bonvoy number, loyalty tier, stay history and open reservations pre-loaded
  • Authenticated guests skip IVR verification entirely
  • Even unauthenticated guests are resolved to a known profile via phone number match
  • NPS improves because the experience feels effortless and personalized

04 · Key Concepts

Understanding the guest identity spectrum.

The solution behaves differently depending on how much we know about the guest. The goal is always to move toward authentication as early as possible.

Anonymous Lowest Context

The guest has shared nothing yet — no login, no reservation number, no phone number. We can still capture what they're doing (pages visited, help topics browsed, chat conversation) but we can't tie it to a known person. The goal is to move out of anonymous state as early as possible.

Identified Partial Context

A step below full authentication — the guest hasn't logged in, but they've shared enough (a phone number, reservation confirmation, or email) to be matched to a known record. The system can now connect their current session to their Bonvoy profile, even without a formal sign-in.

Authenticated Full Context

The guest has proved who they are — typically by logging into their Bonvoy account. Once authenticated, Salesforce immediately knows their full history, preferences, stays, and profile. Think of it as the digital equivalent of a hotel recognizing a returning guest by name at check-in.

Personalization The Outcome

Using everything we know — loyalty tier, stay history, preferences, current reservation, and what the guest was just browsing — to make the experience feel tailored. The agent never asks a question the guest already answered. Their first words feel relevant, not generic.

05 · Phased Approach

Two phases to the full solution.

Phase 1 solves the core problem: making the two systems talk to each other and providing a better experience. Phase 2 elevates what the agent sees and deepens the intelligence.

Phase 1 · Now

Make the systems communicate.
  • Unified Web SDK capturing anonymous behavioral events from the Help site
  • Click-to-call button that captures or verifies guest phone number
  • Data Cloud identity resolution — match phone to Bonvoy profile
  • Context payload: conversation summary, intent, and key data passed to agent
  • Outbound call trigger via Salesforce Voice / AFCC
  • Agent receives guest intent before picking up — no "How can I help?"
  • Skip IVR for authenticated guests entirely

Phase 2 · Next

Deepen the agent experience.
  • Full agent screen pop with complete Bonvoy profile, stay history, and open cases
  • NICE Contact Center data integration — call disposition and handle time
  • Recognition of where the guest left off: "I saw you want to make a name change, let me pick up from there"
  • NICL Unification data ingestion for deeper identity enrichment
  • Tableau NPS correlation dashboard — measure impact on guest satisfaction
  • Agentforce Voice for autonomous resolution before live agent transfer
